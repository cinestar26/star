In a high-voltage IPL Eliminator at the M. Chinnaswamy Stadium, Royal Challengers Bengaluru edged past Chennai Super Kings by 4 runs in a last-ball thriller. Chasing a mammoth 211, CSK needed 18 off the final over, but a sensational bowling display from Siraj and a stunning catch at long-on turned the game on its head. The atmosphere was electric as 38,000 fans witnessed a classic rivalry renewed. This victory sends RCB to Qualifier 2, while CSK's valiant effort fell just short despite Jadeja's all-round heroics.

RCB’s innings was built around a captain's knock from Virat Kohli, who remained unbeaten on 89 off 49 balls, laced with 8 fours and 4 sixes. He found able support from Faf du Plessis (42 off 27) and a late cameo from Maxwell (22 off 9). CSK’s bowlers struggled to contain the run flow on a batting paradise, with only Jadeja providing breakthroughs. The target of 211 seemed daunting, but Chennai's top-order started aggressively, racing to 68/1 in the powerplay. The stage was set for an epic chase.

CSK needed 58 runs off the last 4 overs with Dhoni and Jadeja at the crease. The equation came down to 28 off the final two overs. Mohammed Siraj bowled a stunning 19th over, conceding just 5 runs and taking the key wicket of Jadeja. With 23 needed off the last over, Dhoni smashed two massive sixes off the first two balls, bringing the requirement down to 11 off 4 balls. But a sensational low full toss resulted in a catch at deep mid-wicket, and CSK fell agonizingly short. RCB players erupted in joy as the crowd roared.

Player of the Match Virat Kohli said after the game: "These are the moments you live for. The crowd, the pressure, and the rivalry — it’s what makes IPL the greatest league. Credit to the bowlers for holding nerve. We are not done yet." Meanwhile, MS Dhoni remained philosophical, highlighting the fighting spirit of his team. Social media exploded with #RCBvCSK trending worldwide for over 8 hours. The match will be remembered as one of the greatest IPL thrillers in recent history.
